DRILLING
Operation: Drilling creates cylindrical holes using a rotating cutting tool.
Tolerance achievable: IT11 to IT12 without further operations.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 6.3 to 12.5 micrometres.
Standard cutting speed for steel: 20 to 30 m/min with HSS drill.
Standard cutting speed for steel: 60 to 90 m/min with carbide drill.
Feed rate: 0.1 to 0.3 mm/rev depending on diameter.
Setup time: 5 minutes average for CNC machining centre.
Machine required: CNC machining centre or drill press.

REAMING
Operation: Reaming improves hole accuracy and surface finish after drilling.
Tolerance achievable: IT7 to IT9.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 0.8 to 1.6 micrometres.
Allowance before reaming: leave 0.2 to 0.5mm on diameter for reaming.
Cutting speed: one third of drilling speed for same material.
Setup time: 3 minutes additional after drilling setup.
Machine required: same machine as drilling, CNC machining centre.

BORING
Operation: Boring enlarges and corrects existing holes to tight tolerances.
Tolerance achievable: IT6 to IT8.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 0.4 to 1.6 micrometres.
Used when: hole requires better position accuracy than drilling provides.
Used when: tolerance class IT7 or better is required.
Setup time: 15 minutes for boring bar setup on CNC machining centre.
Machine required: CNC machining centre with boring bar.

FACE MILLING
Operation: Face milling produces flat surfaces using a face mill cutter.
Tolerance achievable: IT8 to IT10 for flatness.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 0.8 to 3.2 micrometres.
Cutting speed for steel: 100 to 200 m/min with carbide inserts.
Feed per tooth: 0.1 to 0.3 mm per tooth.
Setup time: 8 minutes average.
Machine required: CNC machining centre.

END MILLING / SLOT MILLING
Operation: End milling produces slots, pockets, keyways, and contoured surfaces.
Tolerance achievable: IT9 to IT11 for width.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 1.6 to 3.2 micrometres.
Cutting speed for steel: 80 to 150 m/min with carbide end mill.
Setup time: 10 minutes average.
Machine required: CNC machining centre.

CNC TURNING
Operation: Turning produces cylindrical, conical, and faced surfaces on a lathe.
Tolerance achievable: IT6 to IT8 for diameter.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 0.4 to 1.6 micrometres.
Cutting speed for steel: 150 to 300 m/min with carbide insert.
Feed rate: 0.1 to 0.3 mm/rev for finishing cuts.
Setup time: 12 minutes average for CNC lathe.
Machine required: CNC lathe.

TAPPING
Operation: Tapping cuts internal threads using a tap tool.
Always preceded by drilling a tap drill hole.
Tap drill diameter: nominal thread diameter minus pitch.
Example M20x1.5: tap drill = 20 - 1.5 = 18.5mm diameter.
Setup time: 5 minutes additional after drilling.
Machine required: CNC machining centre with rigid tapping cycle.

GRINDING
Operation: Grinding achieves very tight tolerances and fine surface finishes.
Tolerance achievable: IT5 to IT6.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 0.1 to 0.4 micrometres.
Used when: tolerance better than IT6 is required.
Used when: surface finish Ra better than 0.4 micrometres required.
Setup time: 20 minutes average.
Machine required: cylindrical grinder or surface grinder.

HONING
Operation: Honing improves bore geometry and surface finish after boring or grinding.
Tolerance achievable: IT5 to IT6 for bore diameter.
Surface finish achievable: Ra 0.1 to 0.8 micrometres.
Setup time: 15 minutes.
Machine required: honing machine.

CHAMFERING
Operation: Chamfering removes sharp edges at hole entries and part edges.
No tolerance class assigned — cosmetic and functional deburring.
Surface finish: same as parent operation.
Cycle time: 1 to 2 minutes per chamfer.
Setup time: 3 minutes.
Machine required: CNC machining centre with chamfer mill.
